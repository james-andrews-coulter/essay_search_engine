Good Listening

We have it drummed into us from an early stage that good relationships rely on both partners spending a lot of time listening to one another. As a result, almost all lovers feel in their hearts, with perhaps a touch of weariness, that they have put in a good number of hours listening to their companions: their stories, their pet schemes, their views on the world, their particular irritations with this or that facet of existence … And yet, despite both a theoretical respect for the idea of listening and all the many occasions when we have technically been taking in what our partners have been telling us, there is still nothing more common than for people in relationships to report feeling that they have not been properly listened to. Feeling heard is evidently a good deal more complicated than being heard.

Conclusion

We would, of course, like any encounter with mental unwellness to be as brief as possible and, most importantly, to be isolated and singular. But the reality is that for many of us the illness will threaten to return for visits throughout our lives; it is a condition to which we will be permanently susceptible. So the challenge isn’t to learn to survive only a one-off crisis but to set in place a framework that can help us manage our fragility over the long term.

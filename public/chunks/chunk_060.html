Dignity

We may long for the seriousness and beauty of religion. In a house of prayer, the architecture directs the eye to eternity, the music summons the most serious parts of one’s soul. There is an invitation to be wholly pure. In the temples of religion, we can at last leave behind the usual pettiness, squalor and our own more repulsive appetites. This is the true home from which we have been in exile.

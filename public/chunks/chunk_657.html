¶ Procrastination

Procrastination is often mistaken for laziness, but is really a species of terror at the possible consequences of messing up. The best way to address the disease is to reduce the imaginative spectre of failure and gently raise the spectre of inaction, which is never cost-free. We get down to work when the fear of doing nothing at all finally exceeds the fear of doing it badly.

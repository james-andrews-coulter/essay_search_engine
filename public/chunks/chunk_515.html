London, England, mid-nineteenth century

Work of all kinds has become sacred; our toil pleases God. To be idle is a vice, to accumulate money is a great virtue. The potent demand for ‘respectability’ makes people spend even their leisure hours reading improving books. Everyone starts to complain that they don’t have time any more. In the large cities, a frenetic spirit takes hold. A new ideology implies that everyone could restart the world from scratch every morning – or should feel bad for not doing so.

Ford Madox Brown, Work, started 1852 and completed 1865(ironically, the artist procrastinated for thirteen years over finishing his homage to strenuous activity).

3When we realise we are crazy

This is deeply counter-intuitive. We seem so normal and mostly so good. It’s the others who are the crazy ones.

But maturity is founded on an active sense of one’s own folly. One is out of control for long periods; one has failed to master one’s past; one projects unhelpfully; one is permanently anxious. One is, to put it mildly, an idiot.

If we are not regularly and very deeply embarrassed by who we are, it can only be because we haven’t begun to understand our own narrative.
